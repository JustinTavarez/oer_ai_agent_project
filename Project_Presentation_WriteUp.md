# Why I Chose the OER AI Agent as My Project Topic

## Introduction

For my presentation, I chose to build and showcase the **OER AI Agent**, a full-stack web application that leverages artificial intelligence to help students and educators discover Open Educational Resources (OER). This project sits at the intersection of two areas I am deeply passionate about: making education more accessible and exploring the practical applications of AI in everyday problem-solving.

## Why This Topic Is Important

The rising cost of textbooks and course materials remains one of the most significant financial barriers students face in higher education. According to multiple studies, students routinely spend hundreds of dollars per semester on required materials, and many are forced to choose between buying textbooks and covering other essential expenses. Open Educational Resources offer a powerful solution to this problem. OER materials are freely available, openly licensed educational content that anyone can use, adapt, and redistribute. However, despite the growing availability of OER, discovering the right resources for a specific course or learning objective can be surprisingly difficult. Traditional keyword-based search often returns irrelevant or overwhelming results, leaving students and instructors frustrated.

This is exactly the problem the OER AI Agent was designed to solve. By combining a conversational AI interface with curated OER data sources, the application allows users to describe what they need in plain language and receive tailored, high-quality recommendations. Instead of sifting through dozens of search results, a student can simply ask, "I need free materials for my English 1101 course on argumentative writing," and receive a curated list of vetted resources complete with license information, quality summaries, and suggestions for how instructors might integrate them into their courses.

## What I Want to Learn

Through this project, I wanted to deepen my understanding of several key areas. First, I wanted to explore how large language models can be integrated into real-world applications using frameworks like FastAPI and LM Studio's OpenAI-compatible API. Second, I wanted to gain hands-on experience building modern, responsive front-end interfaces with React, Vite, and Tailwind CSS. Third, I was eager to learn about prompt engineering and how to design system prompts that consistently produce structured, useful output from AI models. Finally, I wanted to understand the full-stack development workflow, from designing RESTful APIs and managing cross-origin requests to deploying a polished user experience with animations and real-time status indicators.

## What I Want to Share with the Audience

I want the audience to walk away with an appreciation for how AI can be applied to meaningful, real-world challenges beyond the usual chatbot demonstrations. I also want to share the technical architecture behind the application, showing how a React front end communicates with a FastAPI backend, which in turn interacts with a locally hosted language model. I hope to inspire others to think creatively about how AI tools can be used to reduce costs, improve access, and enhance the learning experience for all students.

## What Will Be Showcased in the Presentation

The presentation will include a live demonstration of the OER AI Agent, walking through the user journey from the landing page to the chat interface. I will showcase how the application filters resources by course and source, how AI-generated recommendations are displayed as structured resource cards, and how the system monitors backend and model connectivity in real time. I will also present the underlying technical architecture, including the component structure of the React front end, the FastAPI routing and service layers, and the prompt engineering that drives the AI recommendations. Finally, I will discuss challenges encountered during development, lessons learned, and potential future enhancements such as database-backed resource indexing and user authentication.
